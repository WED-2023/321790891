Q&A

1. List three main differences between web1, web2, and web3

Web1, Web2, and Web3 represent different stages in the evolution of the World Wide Web. The main differences between them include:

  • Web1: Known as the "read-only" web, Web1 was primarily static and focused on displaying information. Websites were simple and lacked interactivity. Users could only consume content but couldn't actively participate in its creation or modification.
  • Web2: Referred to as the "read-write" web, Web2 introduced dynamic and interactive features. It facilitated user-generated content, social networking, and collaboration. Websites became more interactive and engaging, enabling users to contribute, share, and interact with each other's content.
  • Web3: Also known as the "semantic" web, Web3 aims to enhance the web's capabilities by adding context and meaning to information. It focuses on machine-to-machine communication, decentralized data storage, and the integration of artificial intelligence. Web3 envisions a more intelligent and autonomous web, where data is interconnected and can be interpreted by machines.

2. Explain the differences mentioned in question 1 by an example of a use case

Let's consider a use case of searching for information about "artificial intelligence" across different web stages:

  • Web1: In the Web1 era, you might find static web pages with basic information about artificial intelligence, such as definitions and historical background. These pages are limited to displaying text and images without any interactive features.
  • Web2: With the advent of Web2, your search results would include not only informative articles but also user-generated content like blog posts, forum discussions, and social media conversations about artificial intelligence. You could engage with other users, share your thoughts, and contribute to the ongoing discourse.
  • Web3: In the Web3 landscape, your search experience would be more personalized and context-aware. The search engine would analyze your preferences, behavior, and social connections to deliver highly relevant and tailored results. Moreover, it might utilize semantic web technologies to understand the meaning of your query and provide more intelligent answers, such as recommending AI-powered tools or connecting you with experts in the field.
